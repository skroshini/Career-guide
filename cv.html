Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure like buildings, roads, bridges, dams, and water systems.

Key Areas of Study

(The main subjects and topics you will typically learn during your course)

Structural Engineering

Design and analysis of buildings, bridges, and other structures.

Transportation Engineering

Design, construction, and maintenance of roads, highways, and railways.

Hydraulic Engineering

Study of water flow, dams, canals, and water distribution systems.

Geotechnical Engineering

Soil mechanics, foundation design, and earth structures.

Environmental Engineering

Waste management, pollution control, and sustainable development.

Urban Planning

Planning and designing sustainable cities and communities.

Skills You Will Gain

  • Structural design and analysis
  • Surveying and site analysis
  • Project management
  • Knowledge of construction materials
  • Environmental assessment
  • CAD and design software proficiency

Best for Students Who

  • Enjoy designing and building things
  • Have strong problem-solving skills
  • Are interested in infrastructure and development
  • Like working on large-scale projects

How to Get Into This Field (After 10th)

Complete 10th Grade
↓
Choose Science Stream in 11th-12th (PCM)
OR Diploma in Civil Engineering
↓
Appear for Entrance Exams (JEE Main, State CET, etc.)
↓
Complete B.Tech / B.E. in Civil Engineering
↓
Gain Internship / Work Experience
↓
Start Career or Pursue Higher Studies

Further Studies

  • M.Tech / M.E. in Civil Specializations
  • Masters in Urban Planning
  • MBA in Project Management
  • PhD in Civil Engineering
