Tourism & Hospitality

Tourism is about travel, destinations, and experiences ‚Äì including planning trips, managing tours, and promoting cultural or leisure travel.

Hospitality is about guest services ‚Äì managing hotels, resorts, restaurants, catering, events, and customer experiences.

Together, Tourism & Hospitality form a combined career field that blends travel management with guest services. They are interdependent: tourism brings travelers, while hospitality ensures their comfort and satisfaction.

Difference between Tourism and Hospitality

Tourism = Focuses on moving people (travel, destinations, experiences).

Hospitality = Focuses on serving people (accommodation, food, events).

Best for students who

  • Enjoy traveling and meeting new people
  • Have strong communication and social skills
  • Are interested in customer service, events, or global business
  • Can adapt to dynamic and multicultural environments

Not suitable for students who

  • Prefer desk jobs with little interaction
  • Do not enjoy social or service-oriented roles
  • Struggle with irregular hours or travel-related work
