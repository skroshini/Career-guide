Arts

Arts (also called Humanities) is a field of study that focuses on human society, culture, history, philosophy, and creativity. It deals with critical thinking, communication, social awareness, and exploring human experiences.

Key Areas of Study

(the main subjects and topics that u will typically learn during ur course)

History

The study of past events, civilizations, and cultures.

Political Science

The study of government systems, political behavior, and public policies.

Geography

The study of Earth’s landscapes, environments, and human interactions with them.

Economics

The study of production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services.

Psychology

The scientific study of the human mind, emotions, and behavior.

Sociology

The study of society, social institutions, and human relationships.

Philosophy

The study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, and ethics.

Literature / Languages

The study of written works, communication, and linguistic expression.

Fine Arts

The creative expression of ideas through visual arts, music, dance, or theatre.

Skills You Will Gain

  • Critical thinking & analysis
  • Communication & public speaking
  • Research and writing skills
  • Creativity & imagination
  • Social and cultural awareness
  • Problem-solving and decision-making
  • Emotional intelligence & empathy

Career Opportunities

  • Civil Services (IAS, IPS, IFS)
  • Lawyer / Judge / Legal Advisor
  • Journalist / Editor / Writer
  • Psychologist / Counselor
  • Historian / Archivist
  • Economist / Policy Analyst
  • Social Worker / NGO Professional
  • Teacher / Professor
  • Artist / Performer / Designer
  • International Relations / Diplomacy

Best for students who

  • Enjoy reading, writing, and debating
  • Have strong communication and analytical skills
  • Are interested in history, culture, society, or politics
  • Want careers in law, civil services, teaching, or media
  • Prefer theory, discussion, and research over lab-based studies

Not suitable for students who

  • Prefer practical/technical fields (like engineering/medicine)
  • Do not enjoy theory, reading, or essay writing
  • Find social sciences and humanities less engaging
  • Want only high-paying jobs in technology or finance without interest in arts subjects

    • Courses in Arts

    1. Undergraduate (UG)
      • B.A. (Bachelor of Arts) in: History, Political Science, Economics, Psychology, English, Sociology, Philosophy, Fine Arts, Journalism, etc.
      • BFA (Bachelor of Fine Arts)
      • BJMC (Bachelor of Journalism & Mass Communication)
      • B.Ed. (for Teaching)
    2. Postgraduate (PG)
      • M.A. (Master of Arts) in all specializations
      • MFA (Master of Fine Arts)
      • MJMC (Master of Journalism & Mass Communication)
      • M.Ed.
    3. Doctoral (Ph.D.)
      • Ph.D. in History, Political Science, Psychology, Sociology, Economics, etc.
      • Ph.D. in Fine Arts / Performing Arts
      • Research in International Relations, Cultural Studies, etc.
