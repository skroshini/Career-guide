Government & Public Administration

Government and Public Administration is the field that focuses on managing public policies, governance, administration of government services, and working for the welfare of society through leadership, planning, and policy implementation.

Difference between Government and Public Administration:

Government is the system or body of people that governs a country, state, or community.

Public Administration is the practical implementation and management of government policies, programs, and services.

Key Areas of Study

(the main subjects and topics that u will typically learn during ur course)

Political Science

Study of government systems, policies, and political behavior.

Public Policy

Designing, analyzing, and evaluating policies.

Public Administration

Management of government institutions and programs

Economics

Understanding economic systems and development planning.

International Relations

Diplomacy, foreign affairs, and global governance.

Law & Governance

Legal systems, constitutional law, and public laws.

Management & Leadership

Administrative and decision-making skills.

Skills You Will Gain

  • Policy analysis and implementation
  • Leadership and decision-making
  • Strategic planning and governance
  • Public speaking and communication
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking
  • Data interpretation for policy decisions
  • Teamwork and negotiation

    Best for students who

  • Have interest in governance, politics, and public issues
  • Enjoy working with communities and policy-making
  • Have strong communication and leadership skills
  • Want to make a difference in society
  • Are comfortable with research, writing, and analysis

    • Not suitable for students who

  • Prefer purely technical or lab-based careers
  • Do not enjoy social sciences or public interaction
  • Avoid debates, negotiations, or decision-making
  • Dislike bureaucracy, rules, and structured environments

    After 10th Choose Humanities/Arts stream (Political Science, Economics, History, Sociology, English, Geography) OR Commerce/Science students can also switch later

    After 12th Undergraduation

    Undergraduation BA Political Science, BA Public Administration, BA International Relations, BA Economics (Public Policy specialization), BA Governance & Policy Studies etc.

    Postgraduation MA Public Administration, MA Political Science, Master of Public Policy (MPP), Master of Public Administration (MPA), MA International Relations / Diplomacy, MBA in Public Policy etc

    Entrance & Competitive Exams PSC Civil Services (IAS, IPS, IFS, IRS), State PSC exams (Provincial Civil Services), SSC CGL (for administrative jobs), UGC-NET/JRF (for lectureship/research), Foreign Service / UN recruitment exams, GRE/GMAT + TOEFL/IELTS (for international MPA/MPP)
